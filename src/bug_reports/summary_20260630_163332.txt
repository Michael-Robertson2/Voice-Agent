# Receptionist System QA Report

## Summary
The receptionist successfully created a demo patient profile, gathered the visit reason, searched availability, booked an appointment, and answered a follow-up question. However, there were several issues involving provider-name consistency, conversation flow, patient verification, and appointment confirmation.

---

## Issues & Areas for Improvement

### 1. Provider Name Changed During Booking
- **Issue:** The system first offered the appointment with **“Dugie Hauser”**, then confirmed it with **“Judy Hauser.”**
- **Why it matters:** This creates confusion and could lead to the patient arriving expecting the wrong provider.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Use one canonical provider name from the scheduling system throughout the call.
  - Before final booking, confirm: “Just to confirm, this appointment is with Judy Hauser on Thursday, July 9 at 3 PM. Would you like me to book it?”
  - Add validation to prevent mismatches between offered slot and booked slot.

---

### 2. Did Not Wait for Patient Response Before Continuing Demo Setup
- **Issue:** The system asked, “Would you like to create a demo patient profile?” but immediately continued with “I just need your first and last name to get started.”
- **Why it matters:** The caller was not given a chance to answer the yes/no question.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Wait for the caller’s response before moving forward.
  - Example: “Would you like to create a demo patient profile?”  
    If yes: “Great, may I have your first and last name?”

---

### 3. Demo DOB Was Assigned Without Confirmation
- **Issue:** The system stated: “Your patient profile is set up and your date of birth is July fourth two thousand for demo purposes.”
- **Why it matters:** Even in demo mode, assigning demographics without confirmation may be confusing or unrealistic.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Clarify this is a demo-only placeholder.
  - Example: “For this demo, I’ll use July 4, 2000 as the date of birth. Is that okay?”

---

### 4. Missing Patient Verification Before Scheduling
- **Issue:** The system did not verify or collect key patient details beyond first and last name.
- **Missing items may include:**
  - Date of birth confirmation
  - Phone number
  - Email address
  - Insurance information
  - Whether the patient is new or established, aside from visit type
- **Why it matters:** Real appointment scheduling typically requires accurate patient identification and contact information.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Add a verification step before booking.
  - Example: “Before I schedule that, can I confirm your date of birth and best callback number?”

---

### 5. No Basic Clinical Triage for Knee Pain
- **Issue:** The patient reported knee pain for several weeks, but the system did not ask about severity, injury, swelling, inability to bear weight, fever, or urgent symptoms.
- **Why it matters:** Some orthopedic complaints may require urgent care or emergency evaluation.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Add a brief safety screen for musculoskeletal complaints.
  - Example: “Are you unable to bear weight, experiencing severe swelling, fever, numbness, or did this happen after a major injury?”
  - If red flags are present, advise urgent care/emergency care per approved clinical protocol.

---

### 6. Appointment Confirmation Lacked Some Details
- **Issue:** The confirmation included date, time, provider, and clinic name, but did not include:
  - Full location/address
  - Year
  - Time zone, if relevant
  - Appointment type
  - Arrival time recommendation
  - Cancellation/rescheduling instructions
- **Why it matters:** Patients may need complete details to prepare and arrive correctly.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Provide a fuller confirmation:
    - “You’re scheduled for a new patient consultation for knee pain with Judy Hauser on Thursday, July 9, 2026 at 3:00 PM at Pivot Point Orthopedics, [address]. Please arrive 15 minutes early.”

---

### 7. Awkward / Incomplete Phrase
- **Issue:** The system said: “For sharing that. Do you have a specific provider you'd like to see?”
- **Expected phrasing:** “Thank you for sharing that.”
- **Why it matters:** This sounds like a cut-off sentence or generation error.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Update prompt/template to use complete phrases.
  - Add QA checks for incomplete sentence starts.

---

### 8. Speech Turn-Taking / Chunking Appears Unnatural
- **Issue:** The receptionist’s responses were split across many short fragments, such as:
  - “For example, is this for a routine checkup?”
  - “A new patient consultation,”
  - “an urgent concern,”
  - “or something else?”
- **Why it matters:** Excessive chunking can sound robotic and may cause callers to interrupt or become confused.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Combine related prompts into one smoother utterance.
  - Example: “Can you tell me the reason for your visit? For example, is this a routine checkup, new patient consultation, urgent concern, or something else?”

---

### 9. Forms Answer Was Minimal
- **Issue:** When asked whether forms could be completed before the visit, the system only said they can be filled out upon arrival.
- **Why it matters:** Many practices offer online forms or recommend arriving early.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Provide more helpful preparation guidance:
    - “You can complete them when you arrive. Please arrive 15–20 minutes early. If online forms are available, we can send them by email or text.”

---

### 10. No Final Confirmation of Patient Understanding
- **Issue:** After clarifying the provider name, the system did not restate the complete appointment details again.
- **Why it matters:** Since the patient specifically identified a name discrepancy, a full corrected confirmation would improve confidence.
- **Suggested fix:**
  - After correction, restate all key details:
    - “To confirm, your appointment is Thursday, July 9 at 3:00 PM with Judy Hauser at Pivot Point Orthopedics.”

---

## Positive Observations
- The system appropriately asked for the reason for visit before checking availability.
- It offered first available when the patient had no provider preference.
- It handled the patient’s clarification question politely.
- It provided basic preparation instructions: photo ID, insurance card, medication list, and imaging discs.

---

## Highest Priority Fixes
1. **Fix provider-name inconsistency between offered and booked appointments.**
2. **Improve patient verification before booking.**
3. **Add red-flag triage for orthopedic complaints.**
4. **Make appointment confirmations more complete and consistent.**
