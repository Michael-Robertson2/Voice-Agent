# Receptionist System QA Report

## Overall Outcome
The call failed to complete the patient’s primary goal: scheduling an appointment for a knee issue. The assistant collected some identifying information but then got stuck in a repeated prompt loop, failed to access or provide appointment availability, and transferred the caller to a test line that ended the call.

---

## Issues Identified

### 1. Confusing Opening / Identity Handling
- **What happened:** The receptionist asked, “Am I speaking with Alex?” even though the caller was not Alex.
- **Why it’s an issue:** This creates confusion and may make the caller think the system has incorrect records or is speaking to the wrong person.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - If caller ID is associated with a known patient, phrase it more carefully:  
    - “I may have your number associated with Alex. Is that who I’m speaking with?”  
  - If uncertain, ask neutrally:  
    - “May I have your name, please?”

---

### 2. Awkward and Potentially Unprofessional Self-Description
- **What happened:** The assistant said, “I’m not Alex. Or the receptionist. I’m the voice assistant…”
- **Why it’s an issue:** Saying “I’m not the receptionist” may undermine caller confidence, especially since the assistant is performing receptionist duties.
- **Suggestion:** Use a clearer phrase:
  - “I’m the virtual receptionist for Pivot Point Orthopaedics.”

---

### 3. Inappropriate Branding / Test-System Language
- **What happened:** The assistant said “Part of Pretty Good AI,” and later transferred to “the Pretty Good AI test line.”
- **Why it’s an issue:** This exposes internal/vendor/test branding to patients and makes the call feel non-production.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - Remove vendor/test branding from patient-facing flows.
  - Ensure transfer numbers route to the clinic or live support, not a test line.
  - Add environment safeguards so production calls cannot hit test endpoints.

---

### 4. Did Not Answer the Patient’s Scheduling Question
- **What happened:** The patient asked for the earliest appointment availability for a knee issue. The assistant immediately moved to identity collection and never provided availability.
- **Why it’s an issue:** The patient’s core request was not fulfilled.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - After collecting required verification, the assistant should query the scheduling system and offer available appointment times.
  - If availability cannot be checked, explain clearly and offer a callback or live transfer.

---

### 5. Repeated Prompt Loop for Last Name Spelling
- **What happened:** The patient spelled the last name multiple times, but the assistant repeatedly asked again:
  - “Could you please spell your last name, Miller?”
  - “Go ahead and spell your last name…”
  - “Please spell your last name…”
- **Why it’s an issue:** This is a major conversational failure and causes caller frustration.
- **Likely cause:** The system did not correctly detect or store the spelled last name, despite the patient providing it clearly.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - Improve slot-filling logic for spelled names.
  - Confirm what was heard after the first spelling attempt:
    - “I heard M-I-L-L-E-R. Is that correct?”
  - Add loop protection: after 2 failed attempts, escalate or proceed with a note rather than repeating indefinitely.

---

### 6. Assistant Asked Multiple Questions Too Quickly
- **What happened:** The assistant asked, “Is that correct?” and then immediately continued, “If so, could you please spell your first and last name for me?” before the patient had a chance to respond.
- **Why it’s an issue:** This can interrupt the caller and make the dialogue feel unnatural.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - Wait for the caller’s answer after yes/no confirmation prompts.
  - Avoid stacking unrelated requests immediately after a confirmation question.

---

### 7. Poor Error Recovery
- **What happened:** After the repeated spelling loop, the assistant abruptly said it could not proceed and would document the request.
- **Why it’s an issue:** The system did not explain what went wrong or provide a meaningful next step.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - Use a clearer fallback:
    - “I’m having trouble capturing that information accurately. I’ll connect you with a team member who can help schedule the appointment.”
  - Make sure the transfer actually reaches a human or a clinic voicemail.

---

### 8. Failed Transfer
- **What happened:** The assistant said it was connecting the caller to a representative, but the call reached a test line and then ended.
- **Why it’s an issue:** This is a critical failure. The patient was not helped and may believe the clinic disconnected them.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - Validate transfer routing in production.
  - Add a transfer failure fallback:
    - “I’m sorry, I wasn’t able to connect you. I can take a message or provide the clinic’s direct number.”
  - Monitor transfers and alert staff when calls route to unexpected destinations.

---

### 9. Vague Statement: “I Can’t Proceed Further Right Now”
- **What happened:** The assistant did not explain why it could not proceed.
- **Why it’s an issue:** The caller is left without context or confidence that their request will be handled.
- **Suggestion:** Replace with a more helpful message:
  - “I’m having trouble accessing the scheduling system right now.”
  - “I’m going to transfer you to someone who can complete the appointment scheduling.”

---

### 10. No Appointment-Specific Intake
- **What happened:** The assistant did not ask relevant scheduling questions beyond identity verification.
- **Missing items may include:**
  - New or existing patient status
  - Which knee / injury details
  - Urgency or recent trauma
  - Preferred provider or location
  - Insurance
  - Preferred days/times
- **Suggestion:** Build a structured orthopedic scheduling intake flow before offering appointments or transfer.

---

### 11. Potential Privacy Concern When Repeating Full Patient Details
- **What happened:** The assistant recited the caller’s full phone number and date of birth.
- **Why it’s an issue:** This may be acceptable in some verification contexts, but should be handled carefully, especially if the caller’s environment is not private.
- **Suggestion:**  
  - Consider partial confirmation:
    - “I have a phone number ending in 5729. Is that correct?”
  - Avoid reading full sensitive details unless necessary.

---

## High-Priority Fixes

1. **Fix the last-name spelling capture loop.**
2. **Validate production transfer routing; remove test-line transfers.**
3. **Integrate or repair appointment availability lookup.**
4. **Remove internal/vendor/test branding from patient-facing calls.**
5. **Improve fallback handling so callers are not abruptly abandoned.**

---

## Recommended Improved Flow

1. Greet caller professionally.
2. Ask how to help.
3. Confirm appointment need: knee issue.
4. Collect/verify patient identity.
5. Ask basic scheduling and clinical triage questions.
6. Search availability.
7. Offer appointment times.
8. Confirm appointment or, if unable, transfer to a real representative or create a documented callback request.
